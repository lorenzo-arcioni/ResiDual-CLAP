\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

\paragraph{Baseline.}
The baseline is CLAP used off-the-shelf in a fully frozen, 
zero-shot regime. 
Given an audio clip $x$, the $\ell_2$-normalised audio embedding 
$\hat{\mathbf{a}}(x) \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{\mathrm{proj}}}$ is compared against 
the $\ell_2$-normalised text embeddings $\{\mathbf{t}_c\}$ of class prompts 
\texttt{"this is the sound of \{c\}"}, and the predicted class is 
$\hat{y} = \arg\max_c\, \hat{\mathbf{a}}(x)^\top \mathbf{t}_c$\footnote{Since both 
vectors have unit norm, the dot product reduces to cosine similarity.}.
No adaptation, fine-tuning, or labelled data is used.

\paragraph{Contribution: ResiDual$^*$.}
ResiDual$^*$ adapts the frozen CLAP model by learning a per-component 
spectral reweighting of each attention head in HTS-AT, 
without modifying any pretrained parameter. 

\paragraph{Head Analysis.}
To characterise the internal geometry of HTS-AT, I extract the raw 
pre-projection head outputs 
$\mathbf{H}_{\ell,b,h} \in \mathbb{R}^{N_w^\ell \times M \times d_h}$ 
for all 184 heads and spatially pool them into per-sample vectors:
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{r}_{\ell,b,h}
    = \frac{1}{N_w^\ell M}
      \sum_{i,j} \mathbf{H}_{\ell,b,h}[i,j,:]
    \;\in\; \mathbb{R}^{d_h}.
\end{equation}
I estimate intrinsic dimensionality via both linear (PCA, Effective Rank) 
and nonlinear (TwoNN, MLE) estimators, 
and measure semantic specialization through a cross-modal grounding framework 
that projects each head's principal axis into the CLAP embedding space 
and decomposes it via Orthogonal Matching Pursuit over a textual dictionary
(see Appendix~\ref{app:head_analysis}).

\paragraph{Fitting, Optimisation, and Reweighting.}
For each target stage $\ell \in \mathcal{L}$, block $b$, and head $h$, 
I estimate a PCA basis $\boldsymbol{\Phi}_{\ell,b,h}$ and mean 
$\boldsymbol{\mu}_{\ell,b,h}$ from unlabelled clips via forward hooks, 
and initialise a learnable weight vector 
$\boldsymbol{\lambda}_{\ell,b,h} \in \mathbb{R}^{d_h}$.
The vectors $\{\boldsymbol{\lambda}_{\ell,b,h}\}$ are then optimised 
via cross-entropy minimisation on a small labelled partition.
At inference time, each head output is transformed as:
\begin{equation}
    \mathrm{RD}^*\!\left(\mathbf{H}_{\ell,b,h}\right)
    =
    \left(\mathbf{H}_{\ell,b,h} - \boldsymbol{\mu}_{\ell,b,h}\right)
    \boldsymbol{\Phi}_{\ell,b,h}\,
    \operatorname{diag}(\boldsymbol{\lambda}_{\ell,b,h})\,
    \boldsymbol{\Phi}_{\ell,b,h}^\top.
\end{equation}
Full implementation details and hyperparameters are reported 
in Appendix~\ref{app:residual_impl} and Table~\ref{tab:notation}.
