\section{Introduction}

Contemporary audio–text encoders excel at capturing multimodal 
correspondences~\cite{musicaudiolanguage, msclap}, yet the spectral structure 
expressed through attention heads remains comparatively under-analysed. 
Recent findings indicate that attention heads give rise to highly low-dimensional 
residual subspaces~\cite{low-dimensionalresidual2025, residual2024}, suggesting 
latent geometric constraints that audio-domain Transformers do not explicitly exploit. 
This motivates an investigation into whether analogous structures emerge in 
HTS-AT~\cite{htsat} within CLAP~\cite{msclap}, and whether spectral selectivity 
can serve as an effective adaptation strategy for zero-shot classification.

In response, I introduce \textsc{ResiDual$^*$}, a spectral reweighting framework 
extending the residual-subspace methodology of ResiDual~\cite{residual2024} 
to the audio domain. The method (i) analyses attention-head dimensionality and 
semantic specialization across all 184 HTS-AT heads — with full architectural 
details in Appendix~\ref{app:architecture} — and (ii) yields measurable 
zero-shot classification improvements without modifying any pretrained parameter.
